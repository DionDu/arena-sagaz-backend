                                                                      Alpha-Beta minimax search is a very well-known game-

                                                                      solving algorithm. It performs a depth-first search of the

                                                                      search space while maintaining local lower (alpha) and up-

                                                                      per (beta) bounds on the values a subtree can have that could

                                                                      affect the minimax value of the root. Any subtree whose

                                                                      value is proven to fall outside this range can be eliminated

                                                                      without completely exploring it. In contrast to retrograde

                                                                      analysis, then, Alpha-Beta can prune irrelevant states from

                                                                      search and avoid exploring the entire search space. How-

                                                                      ever, as a depth-first algorithm, it cannot easily detect if a

                                                                      newly-generated state has been previously seen and may do

                                                                      redundant work to determine the new state's value.
